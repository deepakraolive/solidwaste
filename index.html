Welcome to Solid Waste Management

Our initiative focuses on promoting sustainable practices to manage waste effectively. Together, we can create a cleaner and greener environment.

Solid Waste Management Methods

Segregation

Waste is segregated at the source into biodegradable and non-biodegradable categories.

Recycling

Materials like paper, plastic, and metals are recycled to minimize waste.

Composting

Organic waste is composted to produce nutrient-rich soil.

Waste Collection

Regular collection and management of waste ensure cleanliness on campus.

Tips for Management

Segregate Waste

Always segregate waste into biodegradable and non-biodegradable bins.

Participate in Recycling

Participate in recycling programs and encourage others to do the same.

Compost Organic Waste

Compost organic waste to reduce landfill contributions.

Reduce Plastic Usage

Reduce plastic usage by opting for reusable bags and containers.

Educate Peers

Educate peers about the importance of solid waste management.

Dustbin Colors and Their Uses

Green Bin

Used for biodegradable waste like food and garden waste.

Blue Bin

Used for recyclable materials like paper, cardboard, and plastic.

Yellow Bin

Used for hazardous waste such as batteries, chemicals, and e-waste.

Red Bin

Used for non-recyclable waste like wrappers and multi-layered packaging.
